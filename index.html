How to use this script

  • This is a guide, not a word-for-word read. Adapt language to feel natural and conversational.
  • Build rapport first — do not rush to the programme. Malaysians value relationship before transaction.
  • Respect face (maruah). Never make the student feel they've made a poor decision. Reframe, don't judge.
  • Family matters. Many students will consult spouse, parents, or employer. Acknowledge this naturally.
  • Capture Intent to Enrol before ending the call — mandatory field that directly predicts EC completion.
  • Send the EC link during the call, not after. Agree a specific completion date before hanging up.

1

Opening & Rapport

3–5 minutes

Warm greeting & agenda setting

▾

Start with warmth. Use their first name. Do not sound rushed.

Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] calling from Sunway University Online. How are you today? Thank you so much for your time — I really appreciate it.

[Tip: Use their name twice in the first 30 seconds. Smile — it carries through.]

I have about 20–25 minutes set aside for us today. My goal is really just to understand what you're looking to achieve, and see whether our programmes could be a good fit for you. Is that okay?

[Tip: Setting an agenda reduces anxiety. Permission-asking is natural in Malaysian culture.]

Light rapport questions (pick 1–2)

▾
Q
I noticed you're based in [city/area] — are you working from there currently?
Q
How did you first come across Sunway University Online?

→ Tap their answer to reveal your follow-up:

Good to know — what made you click on it? Was there something specific that caught your eye?
2

Discovery — Motivation & Goals

8–10 minutes

This is the most important part of the call. Listen more than you speak. Your job is to understand their world before you present any programme.

2A — Current situation

▾
Q
To start, can you tell me a little about what you're doing currently? What's your role and how long have you been in it?

→ Tap their answer to reveal your follow-up:

How are you finding it at the moment — are you enjoying what you do?
What are you looking to move into? Do you have a specific industry or role in mind?
That's really common — what would need to change for you to feel like you're moving forward again?

2B — Motivation: why now?

▾
Q
What's prompted you to look at doing a Master's at this point? Was there a specific moment or situation that made you think — okay, now is the time?
Listen carefully here. This is your emotional anchor for the rest of the call.

→ Tap their answer to reveal your follow-up:

Has there been a specific opportunity you've felt ready for but something held you back?
Is your employer fully supportive, or is this something you're taking on personally?
What made you choose that direction? Have you already started exploring what that path looks like?
That's really meaningful — it sounds like this matters beyond just your career. How does your family feel about you going back to study?
Acknowledge the family dimension without overplaying it. Many Malaysian students carry this quietly.
That's absolutely fine — a lot of people start here. What would you need to feel sure it's the right move?

2C — End goal: where do they want to be?

▾
Q
If we fast forward 2–3 years and this qualification has made a real difference — what does that look like for you? What's changed?

→ Tap their answer to reveal your follow-up:

Have you done any benchmarking? What kind of range are you targeting?
Is that a company you're already in, or would this involve a move?
Are you planning to start something new, or grow an existing venture?
Is that internally — within your current organisation — or more broadly in your industry?
Credentialing for respect is very common in Malaysia. Validate this without being condescending.

2D — Skill gaps: what's standing in the way?

▾
Q
That's a great goal. In your honest view, what are the 2 or 3 things you feel you'd need to be stronger at to get there?

→ Tap their answer to reveal your follow-up:

Is it more the foundational concepts, or the ability to apply them practically in a work context?
Are you more focused on leading teams, or on being seen as a strategic voice in the organisation?
Let me ask it differently — if you were sitting in a meeting with very senior people in your field, what would make you feel fully confident versus slightly out of your depth?
That's really honest — thank you for sharing that. A lot of high-performing people feel exactly the same way. That's actually something the programme is very good at addressing.

4

Closing the Call — Intent & Next Steps

5 minutes

The close is not a hard sell. It is a natural progression from the conversation you have just had. Your goal: one clear next step together.

Step 1 — Confirm the fit

▾

So based on everything we've discussed — your goal to [restate their goal] and the gap you mentioned around [restate their gap] — does it feel like [Programme Name] is the right direction for you?

[Tip: Listen carefully. If they hesitate, that is an objection to handle. Do not skip past it.]

→ Tap their response:

Move immediately to Step 2 — Intent to Enrol.
What would give you more confidence that it's the right fit?
That makes complete sense — a lot of our students talk it through with their family first. Is there anything specific they might want to know that I can help you with now?
Never pressure someone away from family consultation. Offer to be a resource for that conversation instead.

Step 2 — Intent to Enrol (mandatory — log in CRM)

▾

I'd like to ask you directly — at this point, how would you describe your intention to enrol? Are you ready to move forward, are you still researching your options, or are you looking for a bit more information before deciding?

Intent options to log in CRM
Ready to enrol — move immediately to EC and application steps
Researching options — acknowledge, address key concerns, still progress to EC
Looking for more information — identify the specific information gap, then progress to EC
Need advice — understand who else is involved in the decision and what they need
⚠ Log Intent to Enrol in CRM before ending the call, regardless of outcome. Do not leave it blank.

Step 3 — Send the EC link on the call

▾

The next step is really simple — it's called the Evaluation, and it just helps us confirm that your background meets the entry requirements for the programme. It takes about 10–15 minutes. I'm going to send you the link right now while we're still on the call — can I confirm your email address?

[Tip: Send the link during the call. Do not say "I'll send it later." This is the most critical moment in the entire conversation.]
Perfect. Now, looking at your calendar — when do you think you could realistically complete this? Tomorrow? End of the week?
Pin them to a specific date. Vague commitment = no commitment.

Step 4 — Book the 48-hour follow-up call

▾

What I'd like to do is call you back in 48 hours — just a quick 5-minute check-in to see if you've had a chance to complete the Evaluation and whether you have any questions. Would [specific date and time] work for you?

[Tip: Always offer a specific time. "I'll call you" is too vague and reduces completion rates.]

Before I let you go — is there anything at all that might get in the way of you completing the Evaluation by [agreed date]? Any concerns I haven't addressed?

[Tip: A pre-emptive objection check at this point is very effective. It surfaces blockers before they become a reason not to respond.]

Step 5 — Warm close

▾

[Name], it's been really great speaking with you today. I genuinely think this could be a strong step toward [restate their goal]. I'll speak to you on [date]. Take care!

Objection Handling

Empathise → Reframe → Close

Based on Jan–Jun 2026 data — the six most common close reasons.

Cultural note: Malaysians often communicate rejection indirectly. "I'll think about it" frequently means "I have a concern I haven't voiced." Always explore gently before accepting a soft no.

→ Tap the objection you're hearing:

⚠ Not Responsive / Goes Silent After IC
Empathise
"I understand — life gets busy and sometimes things get pushed back. No pressure at all."
Reframe
When someone goes quiet after the call, it's usually because something came up — timing, cost, needing to discuss with family, or just feeling overwhelmed. None of those mean they are not interested. A warm, low-pressure follow-up often brings them back. Try: send a personalised WhatsApp (preferred over call in Malaysia for re-engagement) referencing something specific from your earlier conversation.
Close
"Hi [Name], just checking in — I know you've had a busy week. I wanted to share that the next intake is [date], and I'd hate for you to miss the window if this is still something you're thinking about. No rush at all — just let me know if you have any questions or if there's a better time to talk?"
⚠ Not Interested
Empathise
"That's absolutely fine — I appreciate you being upfront about that. Can I just ask, was there something specific that didn't feel right, or has something changed since you first enquired?"
Reframe
"Not interested" at the close stage almost always means: found a better alternative, timing isn't right, cost is a barrier they haven't voiced, or the programme didn't feel relevant to their specific goal. Find out which one before accepting the objection. Probe once, gently.
Close
"I hear you — and I respect that. I just want to make sure I haven't missed something that might have made a difference. Was it more about the programme itself, the timing, or the investment involved? If it's one of those, there might be something we can do."
⚠ Can't Afford / Too Expensive
Empathise
"I completely understand — it's a significant investment, and I respect that you're thinking carefully about it."
Reframe
Affordability is the fastest-growing objection in 2026, driven by real cost-of-living pressures in Malaysia. Do not dismiss it or pivot immediately to payment plans — acknowledge it fully first. Then reframe around ROI, not cost. Salary uplift for postgraduate holders in Malaysia averages 25–40% within 2 years. Explore HRD Corp claims, employer sponsorship, and PTPTN postgraduate options.
Can't afford closes: Jan 4.1% → Jun 4.9% and rising. This is a real market pressure, not a pricing problem.
Close
"Can I ask — is it more that the total amount feels too much, or is it the timing of payments that's the challenge? A lot of our students access HRD Corp funding through their employer, or structure payments across the programme. If it's the total investment, I'd love to share some data on what our graduates typically see in terms of salary movement — would that be useful?"
⚠ Chose Another School
Empathise
"That's completely understandable — there are some great options out there, and I'm glad you're taking your time to make the right choice."
Reframe
Don't disparage competitors. Anchor your differentiation in what matters to this student. Sunway differentiators: MQA-accredited, physical presence in Malaysia (support, network, events), strong employer recognition, flexible online delivery with local academic support.
Close
"Out of curiosity — what was it about the other programme that appealed to you? I'd love to understand, because sometimes when we compare side by side, the differences matter in ways that aren't obvious upfront. Specifically around [their stated goal] — I'd love to show you how we approach that."
⚠ Need to Discuss with Spouse / Family First
Empathise
"That makes total sense — a decision like this affects the whole family, and it's really important that everyone is on the same page."
Reframe
This is extremely common in Malaysia and is not a rejection — it is a process step. The right response is to become a resource for that conversation, not to close it down. Offer to speak with the spouse or parent, or to provide materials they can share. Set a concrete follow-up time.
Close
"I think that's the right approach. Is there anything specific your [spouse/family] might want to know — about the time commitment, the cost, the career outcomes — that I can help you prepare for that conversation? And shall we pencil in a call for [specific day] once you've had a chance to talk it through?"
⚠ Does Not Meet Admissions Requirements
Empathise
"Thank you for being open about your background — that actually helps me make sure we find the right path for you."
Reframe
For candidates with a Diploma rather than a Bachelor's degree, check whether Sunway offers a qualifying pathway or recognises relevant work experience (RPL — Recognition of Prior Learning). If they genuinely do not qualify, it is better to be honest now and preserve goodwill than to progress them and disappoint them later.
Close
"Based on what you've shared, I want to be honest with you — the standard entry for this programme is a Bachelor's degree. However, I know that in some cases we do consider strong professional experience through our RPL pathway. Let me check this for you and come back to you — would that be okay? I'd rather take an extra day to find out for certain than give you the wrong answer now."
Malaysia-specific tips
  • HRD Corp / HRDF: Many Malaysian employers can claim training costs through HRD Corp. Ask: "Does your company use HRD Corp funding?" This often unlocks employer sponsorship conversations.
  • PTPTN: Postgraduate study may qualify. Students with existing PTPTN debt may be anxious — acknowledge and clarify eligibility before it becomes a blocker.
  • RON95 / Cost of living: Many students are genuinely stretched. Avoid minimising the concern. Focus on ROI framing and concrete salary data rather than instalment amounts.
  • WhatsApp re-engagement: For not-responsive leads, a well-crafted WhatsApp message referencing something specific from the IC conversation outperforms a cold call. Keep it short, warm, and personal.
  • Religious / Ramadan timing: Be sensitive to major religious periods. Do not pressure close near the end of Ramadan. Extend follow-up windows accordingly.

Quick Reference — Call Checklist

Tick off as you go
  • Opening & rapport — use their name, set agenda, 1–2 rapport questions
  • Motivation — why now? Surface the emotional driver
  • End goal — where do they want to be in 2–3 years?
  • Skill gaps — what is standing between them and that goal?
  • Programme alignment — link their gaps to specific learning outcomes
  • Confirm fit — does the programme feel right to them?
  • Intent to Enrol — ask directly and LOG IN CRM before ending call
  • Send EC link on the call — confirm receipt, agree completion date
  • Book 48-hour follow-up call — specific date and time
  • Pre-emptive objection check — anything that might get in the way?
  • Warm close — restate their goal, express confidence, confirm next call
The IC is a closing conversation, not an information session. Your goal is not to tell them about the programme — it is to understand their world, connect it to the programme, and take one clear next step together.
