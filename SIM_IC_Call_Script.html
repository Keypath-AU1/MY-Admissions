    Objection Handling

    as needed
    ⚠ "Isn't this eligible for SkillsFuture Credit?"
    EMPATHISE
    "That's a fair question — a lot of people ask that, since SkillsFuture Credit covers so many courses in Singapore."
    REFRAME
    Be upfront and honest: these SIM Online postgraduate programmes are not SkillsFuture Credit-eligible. Do not dodge this — it damages trust if discovered later. Instead, pivot immediately to a value/ROI sell: this is a postgraduate-level qualification with a direct pathway to a UNSW Master's, not a short CET course. The comparison isn't "cheap SkillsFuture course vs. this" — it's "career-level qualification investment vs. course-level top-up."
    CLOSE
    "I want to be upfront with you — this particular programme isn't SkillsFuture Credit-eligible, since it's positioned at postgraduate level rather than as a short course. What I'd rather focus on is the actual outcome: this directly leads toward a UNSW Master's at a 20% saving, and that's a very different return than a subsidised short course. Does your employer have a training or L&D budget that could support this instead? CONFIRM current employer co-funding / instalment options available before offering specifics."
    ⚠ "Isn't a Certificate lower than my Bachelor's degree?"
    EMPATHISE
    "That's a really common assumption, so I'm glad you asked directly."
    REFRAME
    Explain clearly: a Graduate Certificate is a postgraduate qualification. You need a Bachelor's degree to even be admitted — it sits above your existing degree, not below it. The shorter duration (4 units / 8 months) reflects that it's focused and specialised, not that it's a lower academic level.
    CLOSE
    "To be really clear — this is a postgraduate qualification that builds on top of your Bachelor's degree, not a step below it. Think of it as a focused, intensive layer on top of what you already have, specifically in []. Does that change how you're thinking about it?"
    ⚠ "Why not just apply to UNSW directly?"
    EMPATHISE
    "That's a smart question to ask before committing to anything."
    REFRAME
    Position SIM as the lower-cost, lower-risk on-ramp: the SIM units are more affordable per-unit than jumping straight into a UNSW Master's, the articulation carries a 20% fee saving on the UNSW tuition once GPA/unit requirements are met, and studying with SIM first lets the prospect test postgraduate study (workload, format) before committing to the full Master's cost and duration.
    CLOSE
    "You certainly could apply directly — but most people find it's a bigger financial and time commitment to jump straight in. Starting with SIM lets you build toward it in smaller, more affordable steps, and if you meet the GPA and unit requirements, you actually pay 20% less for the UNSW Master's than if you'd applied directly. It's the same destination, with a more manageable and cost-effective route there."
    ⚠ "Will my employer recognise an online certificate?"
    EMPATHISE
    "That's an important thing to think through, especially if you're doing this for career progression."
    REFRAME
    SIM is a long-established professional education institution in Singapore with strong local brand recognition; the qualification is delivered online but the award itself is not a "MOOC-style" credential — it's a formal postgraduate qualification. CONFIRM current SIM accreditation/EduTrust status and any employer-recognition proof points (e.g. named employer partnerships) before quoting specifics on the call.
    CLOSE
    "SIM has been a trusted name in professional education in Singapore for decades — this isn't an informal online course, it's a formally recognised postgraduate qualification, just delivered in a flexible e-learning format so you can study around your job. Is there a specific concern your employer has raised, or is this more of a general question?"
    ⚠ "This feels expensive for just 4 units"
    EMPATHISE
    "I completely understand — it's a real financial decision, and it's right to weigh it carefully."
    REFRAME
    Shift away from a per-unit or per-month cost comparison (which invites comparison to cheap SkillsFuture-subsidised courses) and toward outcome-based framing: career progression value, the postgraduate qualification level, and — if relevant — the discounted combined cost of the UNSW pathway versus applying to UNSW directly.
    CLOSE
    "I hear you — and I'd rather you make this decision based on the outcome than the sticker price. If we look at where this could take you — [reflect back their Section 2 end-goal] — plus the fact that this can lead into a UNSW Master's at 20% less than applying directly, does the value start to look different? Also worth asking — does your company have a training budget you could tap into for this?"
    ⚠ "I need to discuss this with my spouse / employer"
    EMPATHISE
    "Of course — that's a completely reasonable thing to want to do before committing."
    REFRAME
    Don't push for an immediate close. Equip them for that conversation and set a concrete follow-up time.
    CLOSE
    "That makes complete sense. Is there anything specific — the time commitment, the cost, or the career outcome — that I can help you prepare for that conversation? And shall we pencil in a call for [specific day] once you've had a chance to talk it through?"
    ⚠ "I need some time to think about it"
    EMPATHISE
    "Absolutely, this is a meaningful decision and it's good that you're not rushing into it."
    REFRAME
    Uncover what's really behind the hesitation — "thinking about it" is often a proxy for cost, confidence, or an unresolved objection above. Ask a direct, non-pushy diagnostic question.
    CLOSE
    "Totally understand. Just so I can help you properly — is there something specific giving you pause, whether it's the cost, the time commitment, or something else? And in the meantime, let me send you the Express of Interest link on WhatsApp now so you have all the details in front of you while you decide."

    Singapore market tips
    • SkillsFuture is the elephant in the room — assume most prospects will ask about it. Lead with honesty and pivot to value/ROI immediately (see Objection 1). Do not let this surface as a surprise late in the call.
    • Grad Cert vs. Bachelor's confusion is your other top misunderstanding — proactively explain the postgraduate level in Section 3, don't wait for it to become an objection.
    • Employer L&D budgets are common in the Singapore corporate market — always ask whether the prospect's company has a training budget or sponsors postgraduate study.
    • WhatsApp is still the fastest way to re-engage a Singaporean lead — send the EC link during the call, and use WhatsApp (not email) for follow-up.
    • Competitor awareness: prospects may be comparing against SMU Academy, NUS SCALE, or NTU professional programmes. CONFIRM current positioning/differentiators against each before using them by name on calls.
    • Cross-border call: speak clearly, avoid Malaysia-specific references (PTPTN, HRD Corp, RON95 do not apply to a Singapore-based prospect).

    Differentiation Guide

    Graduate Certificate vs. Bachelor's degree

    A Graduate Certificate/Diploma is a postgraduate qualification — a Bachelor's degree is required for entry. It sits above the Bachelor's, not below it. Shorter duration reflects a focused, specialised curriculum rather than a lower academic level.

    Why SIM, not SkillsFuture-subsidised courses

    SIM Online programmes are formal postgraduate qualifications with an articulation pathway to a UNSW Master's — a different category to a short subsidised CET course. Sell on career/qualification outcome, not on funding availability.

    Why articulate via SIM rather than apply to UNSW directly

    Lower per-unit cost while building toward the same outcome; 20% fee saving on the UNSW Master's once GPA and unit requirements are met; a lower-risk way to test postgraduate study format and workload first.

    SIM vs. local competitors

    CONFIRM current comparative positioning against SMU Academy, NUS SCALE, and NTU professional/continuing-education programmes before using on live calls.

    Lead source framing, at a glance

    SIM × UNSW ad: confirm the UNSW pathway early, qualify GPA/units, keep language conditional.
    SIM-only ad: build SIM value first, introduce UNSW as an upsell once genuine interest is shown.
